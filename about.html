About FHG Builders

Built by people who
have done this before.

FHG Builders was founded by four principals who spent their careers running large-scale commercial, institutional, and residential construction — the kind of work where schedule, budget, and documentation aren't optional. We came together to bring that same discipline to the Denver market, across custom homes, commercial renovation, and ground-up development. We are not a volume builder. We take on a deliberate number of projects so that each one gets the attention, management, and craftsmanship it deserves.

What we believe

Construction shouldn't be hard.

Most projects don't fail because someone didn't know how to swing a hammer. They fail on management — on the budgeting, scheduling, and communication that happens away from the job site. Our entire firm is built to fix that. One dedicated lead per project. Transparent budgets. In-house design. A team that stays engaged from the first meeting through the year after you move in or open your doors.

We bring enterprise-grade project management to every job, whether it's a kitchen renovation or a ground-up commercial build. That's the standard. That's what raising it means.

Leadership

The principals

Brent Hanlon

Principal

Brent leads FHG's residential and client-facing work, bringing a background in large-scale project management to the luxury home market. His focus is on the part of construction most homeowners never see — the scheduling, budgeting, and coordination that determine whether a project finishes on time and on budget. Brent built FHG around a simple conviction: the rigor that runs a commercial job site should run a custom home just as well. He is the first point of contact for most residential clients and stays involved from the first conversation through the first year of ownership.

Jesse Steinert

Principal

Jesse oversees field operations and construction delivery across FHG's projects. With years spent managing complex builds where the schedule was contractual and the margin for error was thin, he brings a discipline to the job site that shows up in the finished product. Jesse's role is to make sure what gets drawn gets built — coordinating trades, holding the schedule, and protecting quality from groundbreak to final walkthrough. Clients know him as the person who always has an answer about what's happening and what's next.

Dan Tuttle

Principal

Dan brings deep construction and estimating experience to FHG, with a focus on the preconstruction work that sets a project up to succeed. Accurate budgets, realistic schedules, and clear scope are where most projects are won or lost, and Dan's work happens before the first shovel hits the ground. His estimating background means FHG's proposals reflect what a project will actually cost — not an optimistic number that balloons later. Dan also lends his expertise to the firm's development and commercial work, where the numbers have to hold up to investor and institutional scrutiny.

Kirk Bliessner

Principal

Kirk contributes a strong foundation in construction management and the financial side of building, supporting FHG's development and commercial lines in particular. His perspective on capital, feasibility, and long-term value informs how FHG approaches ground-up projects and investment opportunities. Kirk's experience spans the full arc of a project — from evaluating whether a deal pencils to making sure the finished asset performs. He helps ensure that every FHG project is sound not just in how it's built, but in how it's conceived and financed.
